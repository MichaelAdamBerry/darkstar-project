1965 : The Warlocks

The band that would become the Grateful Dead formed in 1965 when Bob Weir met Jerry Garcia in behind a music store in Palo Alto.

Despite never playing the instrument, Phil Lesch, an avant garde composition student was recuited to play bass. And Bill Kreutzman kept the beat.

Ron Mckennan, a blues singer appropriately refered to as PigPen was the original front man and lead singer.

Setlists from the early years are typically incomplete. They played covers of Chuck Berry, The Rolling Stones and called themselves The Warlocks.

Discovering there was another band called the Warlocks, a band based out of New York that would eventually become The Velvet Underground, they change their name to the Grateful Dead in December 1965.

1966

They play as the house band for Ken Kesey's Acid Test happenings in San Fransisco and meet manager Rock Scully and LSD chemist Owsley "Bear" Stanley, who would become their patron, supplier of LSD, and sound engineer.
