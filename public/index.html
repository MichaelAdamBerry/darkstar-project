GRATEFUL DATA

Exploring 30 Years of Setlist Data

A Band Beyond Description

Even for die hard deadheads The Grateful Dead can be difficult to categorize. For the uninitiated, the shear volume of recordings and songs is enough to be scared away. As music critic Tim Sommer sums up nicely: “Loving the Grateful Dead is like ridin’ that train to Hogwarts— you must believe that the track exists in order to climb aboard.”

These visualizations use data from archive.com and setlist.fm to observe both total career setlist data as well as specific setlist data for each year from 1965 - 1995, examining:

  • What songs were played the most.
  • The size of their repetoir - How many unique songs were played
  • When songs debuted.
  • The make up of the setlist by primary lyricist and composer
